### Scenario Commentary

**COVID-19 Parallel** is the most severe scenario for this portfolio. The combined equity exposure (SP500 24% + FTSE100 8% = 32%) drives approximately −£219,200 of the total loss. Gold partially offsets (+£42,000) but is insufficient. The short GBPUSD position benefits from sterling weakness in a crisis (+£17,600 contribution), partially cushioning losses.

**Rate Shock (2022-style)** is the second most severe outcome. Energy (WTI) provides meaningful offset (+£44,000), benefiting from the inflationary pressures that drive such a scenario. Equity losses remain the primary driver.

**USD Strength** affects the portfolio via long FX exposures (EURUSD, adverse) and commodity positions (gold/silver, adverse), but the short GBPUSD partially offsets. Overall impact is moderate.

**Flash Crash** is the most resilient scenario — gold and JPY safe-haven appreciation largely offset equity losses, resulting in a contained −1.2% portfolio impact.

## Risk Concentration Analysis

- **Equity risk dominates**: SP500 + FTSE100 = 32% gross exposure; these positions drive outcomes in the two most severe stress scenarios
- **Commodity exposure (28.5%)**: XAUUSD provides an effective tail hedge in risk-off scenarios; WTI provides offset in inflationary shocks
- **FX net positioning**: Net long USD vs G10 basket. USD strength is mildly adverse to EURUSD/XAUUSD but benefits from the GBPUSD short
- **Diversification benefit**: The GBPUSD short and XAUUSD long provide meaningful offsets in multiple stress scenarios, reducing tail risk vs a pure long equity/commodity portfolio

## Limitations and Assumptions

- Return data covers **30 synthetic trading days** — statistically insufficient for robust VaR (minimum 250 days recommended in practice)
- Stress scenarios apply **instantaneous shocks** — real-world stress events unfold over days/weeks with path dependency
- **Tail correlations** in stress periods typically exceed Pearson correlations estimated from normal trading conditions
- No **liquidity adjustment** — actual losses in stress events may be higher due to wide spreads and market impact
- No **margin, funding, or counterparty risk** modelled
